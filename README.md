1. THE THREE-ACT STRUCTURE (Matthew Dicks Framework)
Based on Matthew Dicks' approach to storytelling, every post follows:
BEGINNING (Hook)

Grabs attention immediately
Often starts with a moment, not an explanation
Creates curiosity or emotional connection
Example: "I used to think data spoke for itself. I was wrong."

MIDDLE (Context + Development)

Sets up the situation or problem
Provides necessary background
Builds tension or establishes stakes
Example: "Picture this: executives drowning in reports, missing the insights..."

END (Revelation + Consequences)

The "Aha!" moment - the insight that changes perspective
The "So What?" - why it matters
Clear stakes and real-world impact
Example: "Here's what the data reveals: [insight]. This means [specific impact on your career]..."

2. DATA RELIABILITY (Evidence-Based Storytelling)
Inspired by Michael Lewis's journalistic rigor:

Every claim must be verifiable from the transcript or reliable web sources
Data sources tracked for each post
Reliability scoring (0-1 scale) for each claim
Transparent attribution - readers know where information comes from

3. AUDIENCE-FIRST APPROACH
Everything is tailored for professionals in their 20s-30s because:

They're building careers and need actionable insights
They value authenticity over corporate speak
They want to understand trends before they're mainstream
They care about practical application

For each post, the agent considers:

Career growth implications
Skills development opportunities
Industry trends that matter now
Practical steps they can take

4. THE REVELATION (The "Aha!" Moment)
Every story needs a moment where perspective shifts:

What people think vs. what the data shows
Common assumptions vs. surprising reality
The hidden pattern that changes everything
